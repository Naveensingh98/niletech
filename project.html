⭐ CASE STUDY 1: Large-Scale Bioremediation of 11 Urban Drains – Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh

Background

Lucknow faces pressure on its drainage network due to high population density and ageing sewer infrastructure. Untreated sewage resulted in odour, pollution and public-health concerns. Niletech Infrabuild was assigned to implement an eco-friendly wastewater treatment solution across 11 major drains.

Challenge
  • High BOD, COD and suspended solids
  • Continuous flow of domestic sewage
  • Odour and microbial contamination
  • Minimal space for civil work
  • No existing treatment system
Intervention
  • In-situ microbial dosing using NILETREAT
  • Bar-screen installation
  • Flow optimisation with V-notch
  • Continuous monitoring
  • Sample testing at CSIR-IITR
Outcomes
  • BOD reduction: 70–75%
  • COD reduction: 68–73%
  • TSS reduction: 68–76%
Impact

Niletech received official completion certificates from Nagar Nigam Lucknow. The project became a model for decentralised bioremediation in India.

⭐ CASE STUDY 2: Ecological Revival of Sarusola Beel – Guwahati, Assam

Background

Sarusola Beel suffered from sewage discharge, silt deposition and heavy organic load. The lake developed turbidity, odour and algae dominance. The authorities required a nature-based restoration strategy.

Challenge
  • High organic pollution (BOD/COD)
  • Shallow depth & sludge accumulation
  • Multiple sewage inflows
  • Algal bloom & odour issues
Intervention
  • NILETREAT microbial dosing
  • Floating Treatment Wetlands installed
  • Coir log placement for solids trapping
  • Guided plantation
Outcomes
  • BOD reduction: ~80%
  • COD reduction: ~65%
  • TSS reduction: ~67%
Impact

The lake regained clarity, reduced odour and revived biodiversity. It now stands as a model for nature-based lake restoration in India.

⭐ CASE STUDY 3: Commissioning of 32 KLD FSTPs – Baraut & Hapur, Uttar Pradesh (AMRUT Mission)

Background

Cities with limited sewer coverage rely on septic tanks. Niletech built UP's first fully operational FSTPs under AMRUT.

Challenge
  • Independent operation from sewer lines
  • Low energy requirement
  • Compact land usage
  • Environmental compliance
Intervention
  • Screening & feeding tank
  • Mechanical dewatering
  • Solar greenhouse drying beds
  • Anaerobic baffled reactor (ABR)
  • Wetland-based polishing
  • Co-composting unit
Outcomes

The plant produces reusable water, organic compost and safe treatment of faecal sludge.

Impact

The FSTPs became milestones in decentralised sanitation, helping cities transition toward circular waste management.
